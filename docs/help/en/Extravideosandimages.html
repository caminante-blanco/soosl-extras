Extra videos and images

Signs in a dictionary ››

A sign can have extra videos and images (pictures) with it. 

The extra videos often say the same thing in a signed language as the notes say in a written language. 

However, sometimes they show what the sign means. For example, for a sign meaning 'run', one extra video might show a person running.

The extra images can be:

  • A photo illustrating the meaning of a sign. For example, for a sign meaning 'flower', one image might be a picture of a flower.
  • A transcription in a writing system. The writing system in these examples is SignWriting (see signwriting.org).

You will find extra videos and images in different places in SooSL Web and SooSL Desktop.

Extra videos and images in SooSL Web

In SooSL Web, extra videos and images are in the rightmost column, under the senses and example sentences. If the column is long, you can get there quickly; just, click the third icon in the bottom-right corner. 

To play an extra video, just click the video. You will see the usual playback controls.

Extra videos and images in SooSL Desktop

In SooSL Desktop, extra videos and images are in the middle column, under the sign video. There is an icon for each one at the bottom of the column. Click an icon to see its extra video or image.


In SooSL Desktop, you can add extra videos and images.
