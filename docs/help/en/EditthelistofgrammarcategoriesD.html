Edit the list of grammar categories (D)

The whole dictionary ›› Grammar categories ››

In a new dictionary project, SooSL has four grammar categories:

  • Noun (for example, a sign referring to a person, place, or thing)
  • Verb (for example, a sign expressing an action)
  • Adj (Adjective, for example, a sign indicating an abstract quality like size, color, or quality)
  • Quant (Quantifier, for example, a number or a word like 'many' or 'some'.)

You can change this list of grammar categories. Click Tools > Grammar categories. You will see this dialog:

You can change the name of a grammar category, add new grammar categories, or delete grammar categories you don't want. 

To do so, click .

Change the name of a grammar category

To decide what names to use for grammar categories, it helps if you already have an analysis of the grammar of the language. If your language doesn't have a grammar analysis yet, find a linguist who can make one. SIL International might be able to help; contact linguistic_services_gslt@sil.org.

To change the name of a grammar category, double-click the name and type the new name.

Click to save your changes, or to discard them.

Add a new grammar category

To add a new grammar category, click . A new line will appear. Type the name of the new grammar category.

Click to save your changes, or to discard them.

Delete a grammar category

To delete a grammar category, click the trash can next to its name.

If any signs use that grammar category, you will see a warning:

Be careful; if you choose (and later choose ), those senses will end up with no grammar category.

After you choose , the grammar category will have red strike-through text.

Click to finish deleting the grammar category, or click to keep it.
